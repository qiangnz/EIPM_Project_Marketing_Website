Project Verifone EIPM

E-commerce Integration and Performance Monitor

About Project

Verifone, Inc. is launching a new global product that will support e-commerce transactions. New Zealand is the first country that they are going to establish this new product - an E-commerce API that Verifone's third party merchants can use.


As the provider of Payment Terminals in over 150 countries, Verifone oversees over 10.3 billion payment transactions every year. Currently, there are over 35 million payment devices that Verifone across the world has distributed. Unfortunately, due to the current pandemic, commercial businesses and their customers were forced to adapt to the new commercial barrier that people often refer to as 'the Lockdown'. To adapt to this new situation and protect themselves from financial ruin, many grocery stores and other commercial businesses have updated their websites to allow customers to purchase their goods and services online. However, to sell their goods and services, many businesses have had to purchase or pay monthly fees to use Hosted Payment Services/Plugins such as PayPal, WePay and ApplePay.

The growing demand for secure online payment services caused by social distancing restrictions and 'contactless' business policies has the potential to become the default payment method while Covid-19 plagues the world. Before the pandemic, the demand for online payment handlers already existed and gradually grew as businesses developed. However, the market demand exacerbated by the pandemic will be here to stay long after humanity has overcome Covid-19.


The Verifone E-commerce Integration and Performance Monitoring (EIPM) project will focus on the testing and the performance analysis of Verifone's API to maximise its value for the Client.

Goals

There are two key deliverables of this project.

  • 01Plan, build and test the creation of an e-commerce store

    The project's first goal is to plan, design, and develop an e-commerce website that can integrate with Verifone’s API. This website will simulate a merchant website which will make use of Verifone’s hosted payment page.

  • 02Performance testing Verifone’s API

    The second goal is to test the capabilities and limitations of Verifone API. This will include performance testing and load testing to produce enough helpful data that the client can use.

 

Products

The development of the website is the primary product that helped the team achieve the requirements for goal one. The client was able to gather some valuable feedback regarding website development based on the API specifications. The website is also configurable, allowing it to be used to test other APIs.



The second goal of the project was to performance test Verifone’s API and analyse the results. A series of performance tests with varying load sizes and times were done in order to generate an average performance level for the API. Later on, the load was incremented in order to identify any degradation of performance. Results were analysed and the dashboard was born! The dashboard was used to present the result and its analysis to the client.
